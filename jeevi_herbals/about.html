A recipe, kept alive by three generations of women.

Jeevi Herbals began in a kitchen in Anna Nagar, with a notebook of remedies my grandmother had written by hand. Sixteen years later, very little has changed.

Nirmala & Jeevitha · Chennai, 2024
From the founder

The real warrior is named Nirmala.

I lost my father when I was two. My mother — Nirmala — raised my elder brother and me on her own. She is, without any hyperbole, the strongest person I have ever met.

She had been taught a way of caring for skin and hair by her own mother, in our ancestral village in coastal Andhra. Slow-brewed oils. Powders hand-mixed in the right proportions. Soaps poured into wooden trays at the right temperature. In 2008, she started making these for friends in the neighbourhood.

People kept asking for more. Quietly, without any plan, a brand began to take shape.

"We didn't want a beauty brand. We wanted to keep a recipe alive."

In 2025 we're relaunching with new packaging and a proper online home, but the formulas are unchanged from what my grandmother wrote down. The herbs come from the same families. The brewing still takes 21 days. My mother still inspects every batch.

Jeevitha · Founder
Sixteen years, slowly

Milestones

  • 2008A kitchen, a notebook, a few friends.

    Nirmala starts blending herbal remedies from her mother's handwritten recipes and giving them to neighbours in Anna Nagar.

  • 2012The first proper batches.

    Demand outgrows the kitchen. Production moves into a small workshop, but every batch is still made by hand.

  • 2017FSSAI & MSME registrations.

    Government certification brings consistency in labelling, shelf life and quality controls. Same recipes — better paperwork.

  • 2021Sourcing direct from cooperatives.

    Direct partnerships with Girijan tribal farmers in the Godavari belt for raw herbs. No middlemen, full traceability.

  • 2025A new look, the old formulas.

    Relaunch with redesigned packaging and our first proper website. The bottles changed. The contents did not.

How we source

From the Godavari belt to your bathroom shelf — never more than four hands in between.

Every herb in our formulary is traceable. We source directly from Girijan tribal cooperatives and small farms across coastal Andhra Pradesh, where the soil is pesticide-free and the air carries the Bay of Bengal.

We avoid distributors and aggregators. We pay our farmers above the regulated market price. We season our coconut oil for 60 days before infusion. We brew oils for 21 days at low heat. None of this is fast. None of it scales easily. All of it is the point.
